# Seat Comparison Concept

## Goal

Allow riders to compare:

- stock seat,
- OEM comfort seat,
- aftermarket seats,
- professional custom seat,
- DIY modification plan,
- add-on overlays.

The comparison must support users who do not want DIY. If the rider wants to buy a better seat immediately, the site should show realistic OEM, aftermarket, upholsterer, and add-on options with price ranges, source links, affiliate disclosure, and honest fit warnings.

## Buy-Ready Recommendation Style

The site should rank options by fit, not by price or affiliate value.

For each option, show:

- best for,
- not ideal for,
- expected benefit,
- likely downside,
- cost band,
- effort level,
- reversibility,
- ride type fit,
- source confidence,
- link or affiliate link.

Example:

```text
Premium aftermarket touring seat
Best for: long-distance riders who want a low-effort solution.
Not ideal for: short sporty rides where movement on the bike matters more than all-day support.
Main downside: high price and fitment/return risk if the shape does not match your body.
```

This language should be direct. No fake certainty and no sales fluff.

## Recommendation Honesty Rules

- Do not say a seat will solve the problem unless evidence is strong.
- Do not hide cheaper or reversible alternatives.
- Do not rank affiliate products above better non-affiliate options.
- Warn when a low seat may worsen knee angle or reduce foam support.
- Warn when a thick pad may raise the seat too much.
- Warn when gel may increase heat or feel unstable.
- Warn when touring seats may restrict movement for sporty or off-road riding.
- Prefer "likely", "may", and confidence levels unless measured data exists.

## Stock Seat Baseline

For each motorcycle, store:

- stock seat height,
- stock shape notes,
- known rider complaints,
- photos if available,
- measured dimensions if owner provides them,
- manufacturer specs.

## DIY Plan Comparison

A DIY plan should be compared against stock seat by:

- added/removed height,
- expected width/support change,
- material stack,
- cost,
- skill,
- reversibility,
- risk.

## Recommendation Output

Example:

```text
For your profile, a thick gel pad may raise the seat too much.
Try 3D mesh or air cushion first, then consider professional foam reshaping.
Confidence: medium, because exact seat width is missing.
```
