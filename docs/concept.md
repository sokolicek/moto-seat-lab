# Concept

## Working Title

Moto Seat Lab

Slovak working name: Motorkove Sedanky

## One-Sentence Concept

A practical multilingual guide that helps motorcycle riders choose, improve, or build a more comfortable seat based on their motorcycle, body, riding style, budget, climate, and DIY skill level.

## Core Problem

Many riders are unhappy with the stock motorcycle seat. Common complaints:

- pain after 30-90 minutes,
- pressure on sit bones,
- numbness,
- sliding forward,
- too much heat,
- poor passenger comfort,
- wrong seat height,
- seat too narrow or too soft,
- discomfort on long touring days.

## Core Promise

Help riders answer:

- Why does my seat hurt?
- Is the problem the seat, posture, riding gear, suspension, or my body geometry?
- Should I buy a ready-made seat?
- Should I add a gel pad, air cushion, or mesh cover?
- Should I modify the foam?
- Should I ask an upholsterer?
- Can I build it myself?
- What should I buy and where?

## First Pilot

The first complete guide should focus on:

- Germany,
- German language,
- Suzuki GSX-S1000GX,
- rider discomfort after about one hour,
- sport-touring/crossover use case,
- practical upgrades and DIY options.

## Tone

Practical, honest, rider-to-rider, not over-marketing.

The site should feel like:

- a workshop notebook,
- a buyer guide,
- a DIY decision tree,
- a model-specific comfort database.

## Important Boundaries

The site should not claim to provide medical diagnosis.

Seat modification can affect:

- motorcycle control,
- seat height,
- water resistance,
- heating elements,
- passenger safety,
- resale value,
- warranty.

These risks must be clearly stated.
