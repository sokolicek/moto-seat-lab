# DIY Seat Requirements

## Before Modifying A Seat

Document:

- original seat photos from all sides,
- seat height,
- rider pain points,
- ride duration before pain,
- rider height and weight,
- sit bone distance,
- passenger requirements,
- original cover attachment method,
- heating or sensor wiring if present,
- waterproofing method.

## Rider Data

Collect:

- height,
- weight,
- age,
- inseam,
- riding style,
- typical trip length,
- summer/winter use,
- pain location,
- budget,
- skill level.

## Motorcycle Data

Collect:

- model,
- year,
- seat height,
- seat base shape,
- seat lock/mounting,
- rider/passenger split,
- luggage/topcase use,
- weather exposure.

## Use Case

Classify:

- short rides,
- commuting,
- weekend touring,
- long-distance touring,
- off-road,
- city scooter,
- passenger touring.

## Budget Ranges

Draft ranges to verify:

- low: add-on mesh, simple pad, basic cover,
- medium: gel/air cushion, partial foam modification,
- high: professional upholstery, custom cover, heating,
- premium: complete custom seat.

## Skill Levels

### Beginner

Recommended:

- removable covers,
- mesh pad,
- air cushion,
- non-destructive add-ons.

Avoid:

- cutting foam,
- electrical heating,
- sewing complex covers.

### Intermediate

Recommended:

- foam shaping,
- gel insert,
- re-stapling cover,
- simple cover replacement.

### Advanced

Possible:

- full multi-layer build,
- custom cover sewing,
- heating integration,
- waterproof membrane,
- passenger reshaping.

## Material Layers

Possible layer stack:

```text
Seat base
support foam
comfort foam
optional gel or air zone
thin smoothing foam
waterproof membrane
cover material
```

## Safety Checklist

- Seat locks correctly.
- Seat does not move.
- Rider can reach ground safely.
- No sharp pressure points.
- Cover is not dangerously slippery.
- Waterproofing is adequate.
- Heating wiring is fused and protected.
- Passenger position remains safe.
