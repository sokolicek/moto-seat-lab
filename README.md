# Moto Seat Lab

Concept-stage content project for a future multilingual website about motorcycle seat comfort, DIY seat modification, ready-made seat options, materials, tools, and rider-specific ergonomics.

## Current Phase

**Phase 0: Concept and content architecture**

No website code yet. The current goal is to define:

- target audience,
- content structure,
- first country and language scope,
- first motorcycle model guide,
- research backlog,
- affiliate/monetization strategy,
- roadmap.

## Project Mission

Help motorcycle riders understand why their seat hurts, what can be improved, what can be bought, what can be built, and how to choose a solution based on their motorcycle, body, budget, ride duration, climate, and DIY skill level.

## First Scope

Pilot scope:

- Country: Germany
- Primary language: German
- Working source language: Slovak
- First motorcycle: Suzuki GSX-S1000GX
- First rider problem: discomfort after 1 hour of riding

## Target Audience

- DIY riders who want to modify their own seat.
- Riders who are uncomfortable after short or medium rides.
- Touring riders who need comfort for longer days.
- Owners of specific motorcycle models looking for model-specific advice.
- People comparing ready-made seats, gel pads, 3D mesh covers, upholstery work, or full DIY builds.

## What The Future Website Should Include

- Motorcycle seat theory.
- Motorcycle type guides.
- Use-case guides for short, long, city, touring, off-road, and passenger riding.
- Bike-specific guides.
- Country-specific buying guides.
- Material and tool comparisons.
- DIY checklists.
- Ergonomic measurement guides.
- Affiliate product recommendations.
- Maintenance and care guides.
